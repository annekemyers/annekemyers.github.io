ABOUT MY LOGO

MEANING

Hands up if you know what et cetera actually means.
 It means and the rest., from the Latin et '(and') plus cetera (‘the rest’)
—which the Latin actually borrowed from the Greek καὶ τὰ ἕτερα ('and the other things').

So my services are proofreading etc.
That is, proofreading and the other things.
And in my case, the other things are editing, typography and CVs.
TYPOGRAPHIC

So anyway, did you know there are two ways to write etc.?
Both are short for et cetera.
You can write either etc.  or &c. 

THE AMPERSAND

Let's break that down a bit more.
The more old-fashioned way uses the ampersand (&).

The ampersand is originally a ligature (a combination) of the letters e and t.
It is a very old shorthand form of the Latin word et  ('and').
It has retained its original meaning since Roman times.

The original letters e and t are easy to see in some ampersands, like the one in my logo.
But most ampersands are very stylised--to the point where the e + t origin is hard to discern.

FUN FACT

The ampersand sign used to be treated as part of the alphabet.
It was recited after the letter z, and it was originally referred to as and.
This is what led to it being called the ampersand.
​Seriously.
